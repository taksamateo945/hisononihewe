The top diet secret of the 5ft3in star is to enjoy dinner early.

‘I eat with my kids every night at 5:15 p.m.,’ she said. ‘I find that I sleep better when I eat early like that. And I eat enough so that I’m not hungry before I go to bed.’

She has sons Camden, eight, and Jaxon, seven, and daughter Saylor, five, with Cutler. They split in April 2020 after seven years of marriage. 

‘My kids love pasta,’ she added. ‘We use brown rice noodles or lentil noodles.’ 

And she prefers whole foods, staying away from anything processed. 

‘The way I eat is a lifestyle,’ she shared. ‘Organic as often as possible, tons of veggies, meat, nuts and seeds, complex carbs like buckwheat soba noodles, berries, tons of fat — I even eat butter.’

Breakfast is scrambled eggs with goat cheese, avocado and sautéed spinach, lunch is a ‘big salad’ and dinner is enchilada casseroles, burrito bowls and poké bowls.
