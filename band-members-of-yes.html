Who Are the Band Members of Yes?

January 2024 · 16 minute read

Yes is an iconic English progressive rock band that has been captivating audiences since their formation in 1968. Throughout their illustrious career, the band has seen numerous lineup changes and has featured a talented roster of musicians who have contributed to their unique sound and success.

The current lineup of Yes, as of February 2023, consists of guitarist Steve Howe, keyboardist Geoff Downes, bassist Billy Sherwood, singer Jon Davison, and drummer Jay Schellen. These seasoned musicians bring their individual talents and expertise to the table, ensuring that the legacy of Yes continues to thrive.

Key Takeaways:

  • Yes is an English progressive rock band formed in 1968.
  • The current lineup includes Steve Howe, Geoff Downes, Billy Sherwood, Jon Davison, and Jay Schellen.
  • Other notable former members include Jon Anderson, Chris Squire, Rick Wakeman, Alan White, and Trevor Rabin.
  • Each band member has contributed their unique talent and style to shape the sound of Yes.
  • Yes is considered one of the most influential and successful progressive rock bands in history.

The Original Lineup and Early Changes

The formation of Yes in 1968 brought together a group of talented musicians who would go on to create a legendary progressive rock sound. The original lineup consisted of lead singer Jon Anderson, bassist Chris Squire, guitarist Peter Banks, keyboardist Tony Kaye, and drummer Bill Bruford.

However, the early years of Yes saw some changes in the band’s lineup. Both Banks and Kaye left the band, and their departures marked the beginning of a series of transitions that would shape the band’s evolution. Steve Howe joined Yes, bringing his exceptional guitar skills to replace Banks, while Rick Wakeman brought his distinctive style of keyboard playing to replace Kaye.

“Yes is an evolution,” said Jon Anderson, reflecting on the changes the band experienced. “It’s a band that’s always looking to create new music and explore new sounds, and sometimes that means bringing in new talent to help us grow.“

This revamped lineup, featuring Anderson, Squire, Howe, Wakeman, and Bruford, quickly found their creative stride. They released influential albums such as “The Yes Album,” “Fragile,” and “Close to the Edge,” which solidified Yes as a force to be reckoned with in the world of progressive rock.

“The early lineup changes in Yes allowed us to discover new musical directions and push the boundaries of our sound,” remarked Chris Squire. “Each new member brought their unique talents and perspectives, and it helped us to evolve as a band.“

This original lineup, along with the subsequent additions of Howe and Wakeman, left an indelible mark on the music industry and laid the foundation for Yes’s enduring legacy.

Yes in the 1970s

During the 1970s, Yes experienced a period of change and evolution, both in their lineup and musical direction. Notable shifts in personnel occurred, with Alan White taking over the drums from Bill Bruford and Patrick Moraz briefly joining as the keyboardist while Rick Wakeman took a temporary break. This dynamic era witnessed the release of iconic albums such as “Tales from Topographic Oceans” and “Relayer,” which showcased the band’s progressive rock prowess.

Throughout the entire decade, Jon Anderson and Chris Squire remained constant and integral members of Yes, providing a stable foundation for the band during times of transition. While other members came and went, their unwavering presence contributed to the band’s distinctive sound and enduring legacy.

One notable departure during the 1970s was Rick Wakeman’s temporary farewell from the band. However, he did eventually return, further enriching Yes’s musical palette with his exceptional keyboard skills. Additionally, Tony Kaye, the original keyboardist who had previously left the band, briefly returned before parting ways once more.

In the 1980s, Yes underwent significant changes, bringing in fresh talent and exploring a more accessible rock sound. Trevor Rabin’s distinctive guitar work and vocals added a new dynamic to the band’s music, while Geoff Downes’ keyboard contributions added depth and texture. The release of “90125” and “Big Generator” showcased Yes’s ability to adapt to the changing landscape of rock music and attract a wider audience.

“We were excited to explore new musical directions in the 1980s. Trevor Rabin brought a fresh energy to Yes, and together, we created memorable albums that reflected the spirit of the times.” – Jon Anderson, Yes lead singer

Despite these changes, the core members of Jon Anderson and Chris Squire remained committed to the band’s artistic vision. Their unique vocal harmonies and songwriting skills continued to play an integral role in shaping Yes’s sound during this era. The 1980s lineup of Yes produced music that showcased their versatility as musicians while staying true to the band’s progressive rock roots.

Yes in the 1990s and Beyond

In the 1990s, Yes experienced yet another phase of lineup changes as the band continued to evolve. Prominent band members such as Jon Anderson and Rick Wakeman temporarily left the group, creating opportunities for new additions to join the ranks. Notably, Steve Howe and Billy Sherwood became integral parts of the band during this time, contributing their musical talents to Yes’s evolving sound.

Despite these changes, Yes remained active in the music scene and released albums that showcased their continued creativity. Two standout albums from this period include “Open Your Eyes” and “The Ladder.” These albums resonated with both loyal fans and new listeners, demonstrating the band’s ability to adapt their signature progressive rock sound to the contemporary music landscape.

As the years rolled on, Yes continued to tour tirelessly, captivating audiences around the world with their electrifying live performances. The band’s dedication to their craft and commitment to their fanbase have solidified their status as a legendary rock band.

Today, Yes comprises a lineup that consists of acclaimed musicians including Steve Howe, Geoff Downes, Billy Sherwood, Jon Davison, and Jay Schellen. This current iteration of Yes continues to tour extensively, delighting fans with their timeless music and exhilarating performances.

With their enduring legacy and ever-evolving sound, Yes proves that their musical journey is far from over. Fans eagerly anticipate future albums and tours as the band continues to push the boundaries of progressive rock and captivate audiences with their extraordinary talent.

Over the years, several former members of Yes have passed away, leaving behind a legacy of their contributions to the band’s music.
